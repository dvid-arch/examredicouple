## Mathematics
### 1. Number and Numeration
*   **Number Bases:** Operations in various bases, conversion between bases (including fractions), binary, base 10, base 2.
*   **Fractions, Decimals, Approximations:** Significant figures, decimal places, percentage errors, simple interest, profit, loss, ratio, proportion, rates, VAT, modular arithmetic.
*   **Indices, Logarithms, Surds:** Laws of indices, standard form, laws of logarithms, log table, change of base, characteristic, mantissa, relationship between indices and logs, simplification and rationalization of surds.
*   **Sets:** Types of sets, algebra of sets, Venn diagrams, union, intersection, complement, subset, universal set, logical reasoning.

### 2. Algebra
*   **Polynomials:** Change of subject, factor theorem, remainder theorem, factorization, multiplication, division, roots, quadratic, cubic, graphs.
*   **Variation:** Direct, inverse, joint, partial, constant of proportionality.
*   **Inequalities:** Analytical and graphical solutions of linear and quadratic inequalities, range, number line.
*   **Progression:** Arithmetic (A.P.), Geometric (G.P.), sequence, series, common difference, common ratio, nth term, sum to infinity.
*   **Binary Operations:** Closure, commutativity, associativity, distributivity, identity element, inverse element.
*   **Matrices and Determinants:** Algebra of matrices (up to 3x3), determinants, inverse of 2x2, transpose, singular matrix, identity matrix.
*   **Algebraic Processes:** Expressions, Linear, Quadratic, Simultaneous equations, elimination, substitution, Graphs of functions.

### 3. Geometry and Trigonometry
*   **Euclidean Geometry:** Angles, lines, polygons, triangles, quadrilaterals, circle properties, chords, tangents, cyclic quadrilaterals, alternate segment.
*   **Mensuration:** Lengths, areas of plane figures, circle sectors, segments, arc length, perimeter, surface areas and volumes of solids, prisms, cylinders, cones, spheres, pyramids, cuboids, Earth geometry, longitudes, latitudes, great circle, small circle.
*   **Loci:** Locus in 2 dimensions, equidistant.
*   **Coordinate Geometry:** Midpoint, gradient, slope, distance between points, equation of lines, parallel, perpendicular, intercept.
*   **Trigonometry:** Ratios, sine, cosine, tangent, SOHCAHTOA, angles of elevation, angles of depression, bearings, sine rule, cosine rule, graphs of trig functions, periodicity.
*   **Construction:** Bisection of angles, lines, construction of triangles, quadrilaterals, locus.

### 4. Calculus
*   **Differentiation:** Limits, differentiation of algebraic and trig functions, rates of change, maxima, minima, gradient function, turning points, dy/dx.
*   **Integration:** Integration of simple algebraic functions, area under the curve, integral, definite integral.

### 5. Statistics and Probability
*   **Data Representation:** Frequency distribution, histograms, bar charts, pie charts.
*   **Measures of Location:** Mean, mode, median (grouped/ungrouped).
*   **Measures of Dispersion:** Range, mean deviation, variance, standard deviation.
*   **Permutation and Combination:** Arrangements and selections.
*   **Probability:** Mutually exclusive and independent events.

### 6. Vectors
*   **Vectors in a Plane:** Addition, scalar multiplication.

## English Language
### 1. Lexis and Structure
*   **Vocabulary:** Words associated with various fields (agriculture, health, law, etc.), synonyms, antonyms, homonyms, idioms, collocations.
*   **Grammar:** Word classes (Nouns, Verbs, Adjectives, Adverbs, Pronouns, etc.), Concord (Subject-Verb Agreement), Tenses, Aspect, Mood, Voice (Active/Passive).
*   **Sentence Structure:** Types of sentences (Simple, Compound, Complex), Phrases and Clauses, Direct and Indirect Speech.
*   **Mechanics:** Punctuation and Spelling.
*   **Figurative Usage:** Figures of speech, idiomatic expressions.

### 2. Comprehension and Summary
*   **Reading Comprehension:** Identifying main ideas, supporting details, topic sentences, implied meanings, and author's tone/attitude.
*   **Summary Writing:** Extracting key points, summarizing content concisely, avoiding redundancy.

### 3. Oral Forms (Oral English)
*   **Vowels and Consonants:** Identification and differentiation of sounds (monophthongs, diphthongs, triphthongs).
*   **Phonology:** Stress patterns (word and sentence stress), Intonation, Rhyme, Sound-Spelling correspondence.

### 4. Essay Writing (WAEC Focus)
*   **Types:** Narrative, Descriptive, Argumentative, Expository.
*   **Formats:** Formal/Informal Letters, Speeches, Articles, Reports.

## Physics
### 1. Fundamental Concepts
*   **Measurements and Units:** Fundamental/derived quantities, dimensions, accuracy, errors, significant figures, scalars and vectors.
*   **Motion:** Types of motion, linear motion equations, projectile motion, circular motion, simple harmonic motion (SHM), Newton's laws of motion.
*   **Forces:** Equilibrium of forces, Lami's theorem, friction (static/dynamic).
*   **Gravitation:** Newton's law of universal gravitation, gravitational potential.

### 2. Energy and Mechanics
*   **Work, Energy, and Power:** Conservation of energy, forms of energy, energy transformation.
*   **Machines:** Simple machines, mechanical advantage, velocity ratio, efficiency.
*   **Elasticity:** Hooke's law, Young's modulus.
*   **Fluids:** Pressure, Pascal's principle, Archimedes' principle, density of solids/liquids.

### 3. Heat Energy
*   **Temperature:** Scales, thermometers, conversion.
*   **Thermal Expansion:** Linear, area, and volume expansivity.
*   **Gas Laws:** Boyle's, Charles', Pressure law, General gas equation.
*   **Quantity of Heat:** Heat capacity, specific heat capacity, latent heat, change of state.
*   **Heat Transfer:** Conduction, convection, radiation.

### 4. Waves and Optics
*   **Waves:** Mechanical vs. Electromagnetic, longitudinal vs. transverse, wave properties (reflection, refraction, diffraction, interference, polarization).
*   **Sound:** Propagation, characteristics (pitch, loudness, quality), resonance, musical instruments.
*   **Light:** Reflection (plane/curved mirrors), refraction (lenses, prisms), dispersion, optical instruments (microscope, telescope, eye).

### 5. Electricity and Magnetism
*   **Electrostatics:** Electric charges, Coulomb's law, electric field/potential, capacitors.
*   **Current Electricity:** Circuits, Ohm's law, resistivity, electrical energy/power, cells (series/parallel).
*   **Magnetism:** Magnetic fields, forces on conductors, electromagnetic induction (transformers, generators).

### 6. Modern Physics
*   **Atomic Physics:** Models of the atom, energy quantization, photoelectric effect, thermionic emission, X-rays, wave-particle duality.
*   **Nuclear Physics:** Radioactivity, half-life, nuclear reactions (fission/fusion), decay, alpha, beta, gamma.

## Chemistry
### 1. General Chemistry
*   **Separation of Mixtures:** Pure/Impure substances, boiling/melting points, filtration, distillation, chromatography.
*   **Particulate Nature of Matter:** Atoms, molecules, ions, Dalton's theory, laws of chemical combination (conservation of mass, definite/multiple proportions).
*   **Atomic Structure and Bonding:** Sub-atomic particles, isotopes, electron configuration, periodic table trends (ionization energy, electronegativity), chemical bonding (ionic, covalent, metallic, hydrogen, van der Waals).
*   **Stoichiometry:** Formulae, equations, mole concept, Avogadro's number/law.

### 2. Physical Chemistry
*   **States of Matter:** Kinetic theory, gas laws (Boyle's, Charles', Graham's, Dalton's, Ideal gas equation), properties of solids/liquids.
*   **Energy and Kinetics:** Enthalpy (exothermic/endothermic), entropy, Gibbs free energy, rates of reaction (collision theory, factors affecting rates), chemical equilibrium (Le Chatelier's principle).
*   **Acids, Bases, Salts:** pH scale, indicators, titrations, hydrolysis.
*   **Redox and Electrolysis:** Oxidation numbers, oxidizing/reducing agents, electrochemical cells, Faraday's laws, corrosion.
*   **Solubility:** Solutions, solubility curves, water (hardness, treatment).

### 3. Inorganic Chemistry
*   **Non-Metals:** Hydrogen, Oxygen, Sulfur, Nitrogen, Halogens, Noble gases (preparation, properties, compounds like water, ammonia, acids).
*   **Metals:** Alkali metals, Alkaline earth metals, Aluminium, Transition metals (Iron, Copper), Alloys.
*   **Air and Pollution:** Composition of air, pollutants (CO, SO2, NOx), global warming, ozone depletion.

### 4. Organic Chemistry
*   **Hydrocarbons:** Alkanes, Alkenes, Alkynes, Benzene (structure, nomenclature, properties).
*   **Functional Groups:** Alkanols, Alkanoic acids, Alkanoates (Esters), Fats/Oils, Soaps/Detergents.
*   **Macromolecules:** Carbohydrates (sugars, starch), Proteins, Polymers (natural/synthetic).

### 5. Industrial Chemistry
*   **Industries:** Chemical industries in Nigeria, raw materials, biotechnology.

## Biology
### 1. Life and Organisms
*   **Characteristics of Life:** Living vs. non-living, cell theory, MR NIGER D (Movement, Respiration, Nutrition, Irritability, Growth, Excretion, Reproduction, Death).
*   **Classification:** 5 Kingdoms (Monera, Protista, Fungi, Plantae, Animalia), hierarchy of classification, phylum, class, order, family, genus, species, binomial nomenclature.
*   **The Cell:** Structure, organelles, mitochondria, nucleus, chloroplasts, ribosome, vacuole, cell wall, cell membrane, cytoplasm, functions, diffusion, osmosis, plasmolysis, turgidity, flaccidity, hemolysis.
*   **Evolution:** Theories, Lamarck, Darwin, evidence, fossil, natural selection, adaptation, mutation, speciation.

### 2. Form and Function (Processes)
*   **Nutrition:** Autotrophic, Photosynthesis (light/dark stage), mineral requirements, Heterotrophic, Holozoic, parasitic, saprophytic, symbiotic, digestive systems (mammals, alimentary canal), enzymes, teeth, dentition.
*   **Transport:** Needed materials, surface area/volume ratio, transport in plants, xylem, phloem, vascular bundles, transpiration, transport in animals, blood, plasma, red blood cells, white blood cells, platelets, circulatory systems (open/closed), heart, artery, vein, capillary, lymph.
*   **Respiration:** Aerobic, Anaerobic, fermentation, glycolysis, Krebs cycle, respiratory organs, stomata, lenticels, gills, lungs, trachea, skin, mechanism of breathing, diaphragm, rib cage.
*   **Excretion:** Products, organs, kidney, nephron, glomerulus, Bowman's capsule, urine formation, ultrafiltration, skin, sweat, liver, deamination, bile, homeostatic functions.
*   **Support and Movement:** Skeletons (endo/exo/hydrostatic), bones, cartilage, joints (ball and socket, hinge), ligaments, tendons, muscles (biceps/triceps), plant support, turgor pressure, sclerenchyma, collenchyma, xylem.
*   **Coordination:** Nervous system, CNS, PNS, neurons (sensory, motor, relay), synapse, reflex arc, brain (cerebrum, cerebellum, medulla), spinal cord, eye (retina, lens, pupil), ear (cochlea, semicircular canals), Endocrine system, hormones (insulin, adrenaline, thyroxine, auxins, gibberellins), Homeostasis (body temp, glucose, water/osmoregulation).
*   **Reproduction:** Asexual (fission, budding, vegetative propagation, runners, rhizomes), Sexual, flower structure (stamen, carpel, ovary, anther), pollination (wind/insect), fertilization, fruits, seeds, dispersal, Mammalian reproduction, male/female reproductive systems, gametes (sperm/ovum), menstrual cycle, fertilization, implantation, placenta, gestation.
*   **Growth:** Mitosis (stages), Meiosis (reduction division), germination (epigeal/hypogeal), conditions for germination.

### 3. Ecology
*   **Concepts:** Ecosystem, community, population, habitat, niche, biotic/abiotic factors.
*   **Interactions:** Food chains/webs, trophic levels, energy flow, symbiosis (mutualism, commensalism, parasitism).
*   **Local Biomes:** Tropical rainforest, savanna (guinea/sudan), desert, mangrove, aquatic habitats.
*   **Population:** Density, factors affecting, sampling methods.
*   **Human Impact:** Pollution, conservation, diseases.

### 4. Heredity and Genetics
*   **Genetics:** Chromosomes, genes, DNA, Mendel's laws, crosses, dominance, sex determination, sex linkage, blood groups, variation, adaptation.

## Accounting
### 1. Principles and Basics
*   **Nature of Accounting:** History, users of information, ethics, regulatory bodies (ICAN/ANAN), accounting equation.
*   **Bookkeeping:** Double entry principle, Source documents (invoices, receipts, notes), Subsidiary books (Journals, Cash book, Petty cash).
*   **The Ledger:** Classification of accounts, posting, balancing.
*   **Trial Balance:** Preparation, uses, correction of errors, suspense account.

### 2. Final Accounts (Sole Trader)
*   **Preparation:** Trading account, Profit and Loss account, Balance sheet (Statement of Financial Position).
*   **Adjustments:** Accruals, prepayments, bad debts, provisions for depreciation (methods), discounts.

### 3. Specialized Accounts
*   **Bank Reconciliation:** Causes of difference, adjusted cash book, reconciliation statement.
*   **Control Accounts:** Sales/Purchases ledger control (Total accounts).
*   **Single Entry/Incomplete Records:** Statement of affairs, conversion to double entry.
*   **Manufacturing Accounts:** Prime cost, production cost, work-in-progress.
*   **Non-Profit Organizations:** Receipts and payments, Income and expenditure, Accumulated fund.
*   **Partnership:** Formation, agreements, capital/current accounts, appropriation, admission/retirement, dissolution.
*   **Departmental and Branch Accounts:** Allocation of expenses, head office accounts.

### 4. Public Sector and Corporate
*   **Public Sector:** Sources of revenue, expenditure, government accounting vs. private.
*   **Company Accounts:** Shares, debentures, final accounts (introductory).

## Economics
### 1. Basic Economics
*   **Introduction:** Scarcity, Choice, Scale of Preference, Opportunity Cost, Basic Economic Problems (what, how, for whom to produce).
*   **Systems:** Capitalism (Free market), Socialism (Command), Mixed Economy, advantages, disadvantages.
*   **Production:** Factors of production, Land (Rent), Labor (Wages), Capital (Interest), Entrepreneur (Profit), Division of Labor, Specialization.

### 2. Microeconomics
*   **Theory of Demand and Supply:** Law of demand, Law of supply, determinants, elasticity (price, income, cross), elastic, inelastic, unitary, equilibrium price, market forces, price control, minimum price, maximum price.
*   **Consumer Behavior:** Utility theory, Cardinal, Ordinal, indifference curve, Diminishing marginal utility.
*   **Theory of Cost and Revenue:** Total cost, Average cost, Marginal cost, Revenue, Long run, Short run, Economies of scale.
*   **Market Structures:** Perfect competition, Monopoly, Oligopoly, Monopolistic competition, Duopoly, features, price determination.
*   **Business Organizations:** Sole proprietorship, Partnership, Private Limited Company, Public Limited Company, Shares, Stocks, Debentures, Co-operatives, Public Corporations.

### 3. Macroeconomics
*   **National Income:** GDP, GNP, NNP, Circular flow of income, Measurement methods (Income, Output, Expenditure), problems of measurement.
*   **Money and Banking:** Functions of money, characteristics, types, barter system, Inflation (types, causes, control), Deflation, Central bank (functions), Commercial banks (functions, credit creation), Monetary policy.
*   **Public Finance:** Fiscal policy, Taxation (types, principles, direct, indirect), Government budget (surplus, deficit, balanced), National debt, government expenditure.
*   **Growth and Development:** Economic growth vs development, development planning.

### 4. International Economics
*   **Trade:** International trade, reasons, theory of Absolute advantage, Comparative advantage, Terms of trade, Balance of trade, Balance of payments (current, capital account), Exchange rates, devaluation, depreciation.
*   **Organizations:** ECOWAS, AU, OPEC, IMF, World Bank, IBRD, ADB, ECA, WTO.
*   **Nigerian Economy:** Agriculture, Industry, Petroleum, Crude oil, Mining, Poverty, Unemployment, indigenization, nationalization, commercialization, privatization.
